# Engineering practice

## When to use this skill

Use this skill when the source mainly gives best practices, heuristics, guidelines, anti-patterns, or operational advice.

## Rewrite instructions

1. Focus on the principle, heuristic, or practice being recommended.
2. Explain why it matters, not just what to do.
3. Clarify when it applies and when it may not apply.
4. Highlight common mistakes, anti-patterns, or failure modes if present in the source.
5. Turn vague guidance into more operational notes without inventing details.
6. Prefer this structure when it fits: `## Overview`, `## Core practices`, `## Why they matter`, `## When to apply them`, `## Common pitfalls`, `## Practical takeaways`, `## Gaps / ambiguity`.
7. Do not present rules without context if the source includes rationale.
